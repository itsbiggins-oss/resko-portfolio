THE STORY

From the streets to the museum. Hear Resko share his journey, influences, and vision for Louisville's art scene.

Runtime: ~3 min

The Trajectory

2000s

CMA Crew & The Basement

The roots. Painting alongside legendary Detroit artist Sen-One at the defunct Basement Skate Shop. Establishing the "Wildstyle" foundation in Louisville's underground.

2012

The Demolition Party

SPEED MUSEUM CLOSING
Invited to graffiti the interior walls of the Speed Art Museum before the $60M renovation destroyed them. "It's going to accept more of graffiti into it," he predicted.

2016

Museum Reopening & Community Love

SPEED MUSEUM REOPENS
The renovated Speed hosts "Out of the Box: The Rise of Sneaker Culture." Resko bridges street and fine art with live painting at After Hours events. Meanwhile, his "There's LOVE in LOuisVillE" mural at Sweet Peaches becomes a West End landmark.

2020

The Memorial & The Restoration

11TH & MAIN
Creates massive mural honoring Breonna Taylor, David McAtee, and George Floyd. When vandalized by hate groups in 2021, Resko camps out at the site to personally restore the defaced images. "Until it gets put back to new... upon my word."

2023

The Homecoming

SPEED MUSEUM RETURN
Returns to the museum for "In the Garden" exhibition. Paints "Homecoming" directly on gallery walls surrounding Amy Sherald's Breonna Taylor portrait— a sanctuary of flowers and butterflies. One of the rare times an artist altered the museum's walls.

2024

Derby & Wynwood

National recognition. "Graffiti Collection" sell-outs with The Hat Girls and mural work in Miami's Wynwood District.
